Can I drive with a bad carrier bearing?

Publish date: 2024-07-06

Answer

While driving, the bearing gives the drive shaft some flexibility; however, if there is too much flex due to a worn bearing, the vehicle may start to have troubles. Ignoring this issue may harm the differential, gearbox, and the driveshaft of your car.

Then, should a carrier bearing have play?

Registered. the carrier bearing isn’t any different from any other bearing. so no, there should be very very little play if any at all 1/4″ is definitely too much.

Additionally, what does a faulty centre support bearing sound like?

Unusual sounds are some of the first signs caused by a faulty or failing centre support bearing. An severely worn or defective centre support bearing may howl or screech as the vehicle accelerates from a standstill. The roaring or screeching may settle down when the car gets up speed.

Career

Mac Davis was first a member of the group Zots. He soon became a famous songwriter and went on to write many hit songs like “Memories,” “In the Ghetto,” “A Little Less Conversation,” “Don’t Cry Daddy,” and “Don’t Get Hooked on Me.”

In 1970, he released his first album ‘Song Painter’ on Columbia Records. The following year, he came up with ‘I Believe in Music.’ The commercially successful album followed this ‘Baby Don’t Get Hooked on Me.’ His self-titled album came up in 1973.

In the 1970s, Davis released the albums ‘Burnin’ Thing’, ‘Stop and Smell the Roses,’ ‘Forever Lovers,’ ‘Thunder in the Afternoon,’ and ‘Fantasy.’ His albums from the 1980s are ‘It’s Hard to Be Humble,’ ‘Forty ‘82’, ‘Soft Talk,’ and ‘Somewhere in America.’

Davis released the album ‘Will Write Songs for Food’ in 1994. He has released eight compilation albums and 38 singles.

On the acting front, Davis was first seen in the 1979 film ‘North Dallas Forty.’ His other movie credits are ‘Beer for My Horses,’ ‘Jackpot,’ ‘Angel’s Dance,’ ‘The Sting II,’ ‘Cheaper to Keep Her,’ and ‘Possums.’

In television, he hosted ‘The Mac Davis Show’ from 1974 to 1976. Davis has hosted several television specials and has acted in many TV shows like ‘Rodney,’ ‘That ‘70s Show’, ‘The Client,’ ‘Webster,’ and ‘Tall Tales and Legends.’

Awards & Achievements

In 1974, Mac Davis was presented with the Award of Entertainer of the Year by the Academy of Country Music. He has been inducted into the Georgia Music Hall of Fame, Songwriters Hall of Fame, Texas Country Music Hall of Fame, as well as Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame. He has twice won the BMI TV Music Award. In 1998, he was honored with a Star on the Walk of Fame.

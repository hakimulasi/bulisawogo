Matthew Perry Children: Meet His Family Members

Publish date: 2024-08-02

Matthew Langford Perry, an actor from the United States and Canada, became well-known for playing Chandler Bing on the TV show Friends from 1994 to 2004. He even won a Screen Actors Guild Award for his great work on the show.

Despite being in the spotlight, he has usually kept his personal life away from too much attention. Yet, people are curious about his role as a parent amid all the fame and glamour. Here is what we know about Matthew Perry’s personal life and his famous family.

Matthew Perry Children

Matthew Perry did not have any children of his own. He was never married, but he had several high-profile relationships with Hollywood stars. He also expressed his wish to become a father someday, but he never got the chance to fulfill his dream.

Was Matthew Perry Married?

No, Matthew Perry did not have a wife. He went out with Yasmine Bleeth in 1995, then dated Julia Roberts from 1995 to 1996, and later was with Lizzy Caplan from 2006 to 2012. In November 2020, Perry got engaged to Molly Hurwitz, who works with books, but sadly, their engagement ended in 2021.

Matthew Perry Family

Matthew Perry was born in Williamstown, Massachusetts, on August 19, 1969. His mom, Suzanne Marie Morrison, was a Canadian journalist and once worked closely with the Prime Minister of Canada. His dad, John Bennett Perry, was not just any dad; he was an actor and used to model.

Perry’s parents went their separate ways when he was just a little tot of one. After that, his mom married Keith Morrison, a Canadian journalist. Perry grew up mostly in Ottawa, Ontario, but he also spent some time in Toronto and Montreal.

His mom and Keith had four kids together—Caitlin, Emily, Will, and Madeline. Meanwhile, his dad, John, had another daughter named Maria with his second wife, Debbie Boyle. He died on October 28, 2023, at the age of 54.

Justin Trudeau posted a tweet about his passing. He said, “Matthew Perry’s passing is shocking and saddening. I’ll never forget the schoolyard games we used to play, and I know people around the world are never going to forget the joy he brought them. Thanks for all the laughs, Matthew. You were loved – and you will be missed.”

Early Life

Charles Kushner was born on May 16, 1954, to Joseph and Rae Kushner. Charles was named Chanan after his maternal uncle, who had died in a concentration camp during the Holocaust. Charles and his elder brother, Murray Kushner, were raised in Elizabeth, New Jersey. Charles also has a sister, Esther Schulder. Their parents were Holocaust survivors who immigrated to America from Belarus (1949). Charles graduated from New York University (B.A.), School of Law of Hofstra University (J.D., 1979). He also earned New York University (M.B.A.).

Charles Kushner married Seryl (Stadtmauer), Kushner. They have four children, including sons: Jared and Josh. Jared Kushner is married to Ivanka Trump, daughter of President of the United States Donald Trump, and he is the senior advisor to the President.

Career

Charles’s father, Joseph, worked as a carpenter, builder to the construction sites. Also, Joseph managed 4,000 apartments that he helped build. While growing up, Charles worked with his father and continued managing his father’s portfolio of 4,000 New Jersey apartments from 1985. Charles was not interested in building instead; he wanted to own apartments and buildings, so he invested in the real estate industry.

In 1985, Charles founded the Kushner Companies with its headquarters in Florham Park, New Jersey, and became its Chairman. Around 10,000 residential apartments, a home-building business, commercial and industrial properties, and a community bank were added to the Kushner Companies. Over 20 years, Kushner Companies owned 25,000 apartments across the Northeast during the peak period of Kushner Companies (2004). Later, Charles moved his business from New Jersey to New York City, where he bought the 666 Fifth Avenue building in Manhattan for US$1.8 billion (2007).

The Kushner Company still runs by Jared Kushner. However, in the past months, Jared distanced himself from the family business because he is busy with his present formal White House job.

Charles’ younger son, Josh, made a name in the tech industry. He launched his first business, a social media network, from his dorm room. However, the business did not succeed. In 2009, Josh co-founded Thrive Capital, a venture capital firm that originally invested in Instagram. Since then, Josh invested in technologies such as messaging app Slack, online payment platform Stripe, fitness app ClassPass, tech-based real estate brokerage Compass, Amazon competitor Jet, cosmetics start-up Glossier, and more.

Josh co-founded Oscar, a health insurance company valued at $2.7 billion as of February 2016. The famous investors are billionaires named Vinod Khosla and Jim Breyer. Josh never took any formal role in the family-owned estate Company. Josh and Jared co-founded Cadre, an online platform for investing in real estate in 2014. After two years, Cadre raised $50 million from big-name investors, including Goldman Sachs, Jack Ma (Billionaire Alibaba Founder), Private Equity Firm (Belonging to billionaire George Soros and Yuri Milner (Russian billionaire Tech Investor).

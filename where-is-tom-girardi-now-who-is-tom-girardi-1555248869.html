Who is Tom Girardi?

Tom Girardi, born on June 3, 1939, is a former lawyer from Denver, Colorado. He co-founded the Girardi & Keese law firm in downtown Los Angeles. Girardi attended Loyola Marymount University for his Bachelor's and Juris Doctor degrees, and he also obtained a Master of Laws degree from New York University School of Law. Throughout his career, Girardi became known for representing clients in significant civil litigation cases against large corporations.

Tom Girardi Career

Tom Girardi had a successful career as a lawyer, graduating from Loyola High School in Los Angeles in 1957 and later earning his undergraduate degree from Loyola Marymount University in 1961. He obtained his J.D. degree from Loyola Law School in 1964 and pursued further studies, earning an LL.M. from New York University in 1965.

In 1970, Girardi achieved a significant milestone by becoming the first attorney in California to secure a medical malpractice case award exceeding $1 million. Throughout his career, he handled major cases against notable entities such as Lockheed Martin Corp., Pacific Gas & Electric Co, Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority, and Hollywood's major movie studios.

One notable case involved securing a $460 million settlement from Pacific Gas & Electric for residents of Hinkley, California, who suffered health issues due to contaminated water. Girardi's achievements led to recognition, including induction into the Trial Lawyer Hall of Fame by the California State Bar in 2003. He served on the Board of Directors and was President of the International Academy of Trial Lawyers.

Additionally, he was appointed as the first trial lawyer to the California Judicial Council, the policymaking body of the state courts. Beyond his legal career, Girardi was involved in Democratic party politics, contributing financially to campaigns, organizing fundraising events, and advising governors on judicial appointments.

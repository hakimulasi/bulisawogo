FAQs about Johann Nepomuk Hummel

Who was Johann Nepomuk Hummel?

He was an Austrian composer and virtuoso pianist born on November 14, 1778, in Pressburg (now Bratislava, Slovakia). He was noted for his transition from the Classical to the Romantic musical era.

Who were Hummel’s notable teachers?

Hummel was taught by some of the leading composers of his time, including Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Joseph Haydn, Antonio Salieri, and Johann Georg Albrechtsberger.

What are some of Hummel’s key compositions?

Hummel’s oeuvre includes eight piano concertos, a double concerto for violin and piano, ten piano sonatas, and his famous Trumpet Concerto in E major .

Did Hummel have any famous students?

Yes, Hummel’s notable students included Carl Czerny, who in turn taught Franz Liszt, and other prominent musicians like Ferdinand Hiller and Sigismond Thalberg .

What was Hummel’s relationship with other famous composers?

Hummel knew many leading composers of his time including Beethoven, with whom he had a complicated relationship that ultimately turned into mutual respect, and he was also acquainted with Schubert and Chopin.

What is notable about Hummel’s musical style?

Hummel’s style bridged the Classical and Romantic periods with a focus on lyrical melodies and innovative piano techniques that influenced later composers like Chopin and Schumann.

Where did Hummel spend most of his career?

Hummel served as Kapellmeister in Eisenstadt and later in Weimar, where he significantly impacted the local music scene .

Where is Hummel buried?
